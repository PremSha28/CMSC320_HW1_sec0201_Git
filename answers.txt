Q3: What is the difference between Merging and Rebasing?

Answer: Merging is a method used when combining changes from one branch into another, creating a new "merge commit" that brings the history of both branches together. On the other hand, rebasing is
a method used to move or combine a sequence of commits to a new base commit, creating a linear history by reapplying commits on top of another branch.
